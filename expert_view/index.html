Experts View

on crypto-distribution

2016 year showed to the world how useful blockchain system can be and how huge are its prospects for developing all over the world and especially in unbanked regions.

Very little resourсes make comprehensive interviews of people, who are bringing crypto innovations into this industry and spread the "new economy" culture into the world, where mobile phones are still a luxury . Humaniq, as a cryptocurrency startup, which is willing to help poor regions, was inspired by the answers we've got from 95 experts of different fields, and wrote a small article, bringing you some food for thoughts...

Each expert

was asked only 3 questions:

Which cryptocurrency do you think is most advanced as a platform for the expansion around countries of Africa, Asia, South America or Micronesia?
The UN experts think that by 2020 95% of planet population will be network connected. Do you agree?
Do you think that the connecting of 2.5 billion of individuals to financial instruments (credit facilities, accounts, etc.) will help to steer the global economy out of crisis?

Alakanani Itireleng

Director at Satoshicentre of Botswana

1. Which cryptocurrency do you think is most advanced as a platform for the expansion around countries of Africa, Asia, South America or Micronesia?

I think for now the best to be used is Ethereum (both its token and private blockchain). Ethereum is still affordable, and if there are enough education resources for the ways Ethereum can be used as a token of value, it can be the currency of choice in Africa. Bitcoin will not be much distributed there, as far as Africa on the whole came up late with Bitcoin adoption. Structures can be built and opportunities created for Ethereum to be uses in Africa (just like Humaniq gives an access to credit through using Ethereum). Ethereum smart contracts can reduce the number of "real estate" disputes, land registration etc., which are common problems in Africa. The use of Bitcoin blockchain is very limited, despite its good quality. Many banks in South Africa are already trying the Ethereum private blockchain, and companies such as IBM already have Ethereum blockchain user cases functioning in Africa. For me, Ethereum has better chances in Africa.

The UN experts think that by 2020 95% of planet population will be network connected. Do you agree?

I believe UN is right with saying that the globe and the network will be even more interconnected in future, and technology is playing a huge role in this possibility.

Do you think that the connecting of 2.5 billion of individuals to financial instruments (credit facilities, accounts, etc.) will help to steer the global economy out of crisis?

This connectivity can only matter if countries are willing to share knowledge, resources and expertise in various areas of life. There is no any difference when we are connected but still not using our various knowledge to deal with the global crisis coming out of poverty, drought, diseases etc.

1. Which cryptocurrency do you think is most advanced as a platform for the expansion around countries of Africa, Asia, South America or Micronesia?

Sadly, none of current crop of cryptocurrencies is suitable for tackling the needs of large population, neither for using it in everyday life. The scalability demand even inside a small country is beyond the capability of most, if not all, existing cryptocurrencies. Luckily, we have developed both on-chain and off-chain techniques for addressing demanding needs of such deployments, but it will take some time to bring them out of research labs into practice.

The UN experts think that by 2020 95% of planet population will be network connected. Do you agree?

Do you think that the connecting of 2.5 billion of individuals to financial instruments (credit facilities, accounts, etc.) will help to steer the global economy out of crisis?

Macroeconomic crises are quite complex, and it'd be a folly to think that cryptocurrency can address such challenges that require global coordination among actors with conflicting interests. That said, cryptocurrencies can help to address many sins of governments, reining in their natural tendency to inflate their currencies, capping money issuance, as well as providing sound mechanisms for obtaining economic measurements that, in a cash economy, would be impossible to carry out. They are, thus, empowering for individuals and governments alike.
